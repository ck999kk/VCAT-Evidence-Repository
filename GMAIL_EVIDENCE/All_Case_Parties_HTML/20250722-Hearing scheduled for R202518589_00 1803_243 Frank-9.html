Attending a Hearing

Your Notice of Hearing will tell you whether you need to join by phone or videoconference.
If your hearing is by phone conference, you must phone VCAT. The phone number and pin number are on your Notice of Hearing. VCAT cannot call you. If you do not phone into your hearing, the hearing will go ahead without you having an opportunity to tell your side to the member.

How to join a phone or videoconference >

Why you should attend
It's important you attend the hearing to present your evidence and have your say. You may be able to come to an agreement with the other party. For example, if you are facing eviction, you may be able to work out a payment plan with the landlord and stay in your rental property.
If you don't attend, a decision can be made in your absence.
Send your documents
You must email all evidence that you want to use in your hearing to the other party and VCAT at least 7 days before your hearing.
